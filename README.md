## About

The Insular Church of Mvindicraft is the sole Western church maintaining an unbroken line of Apostolic Succession. It was founded after the collapse of valid Holy Orders in the wider Western church, when the Lombard King of Italy appointed Pope John I — a man who was never ordained as a priest — and every consecration, ordination, and appointment that followed from his line was rendered invalid. The rival claimants Pope Clement VII and Pope John Paul I of Malta both descend from this broken line. The Petrine See remains vacant.

In response, the faithful of Britain and Ireland gathered under the Pre-Tridentine Insular Rite to preserve the ancient tradition. The First Insular Synod was convened in the City of Armagh, where the twelve Canons that govern the Church were promulgated and signed by every Archbishop, Bishop, and Abbot present. The Church is governed by a College of Five Primates, headed by the See of Armagh as first among equals.

## The Three Liturgical Rites

The Insular Church preserves three distinct liturgical traditions, all contained within *The Book of Common Use*:

- **The Sarum Rite** — A reconstruction of the liturgy of medieval English Christendom. Rich in ceremonial, the principal rite of the Insular Church for solemn occasions.
- **The Rite of Pope Saint Gregory I** — A missionary form oriented toward Southern Europe, inspired by the ancient Gregorian tradition. Gregory sent Augustine to Canterbury and insisted the Church should baptise local customs rather than destroy them.
- **The Rite of Saint Ninnian the Presbyter** — An austere and elegant northern missionary rite suited to the Germanic and Nordic peoples, commended for the new missions in Scandinavia.

## Governance

The Church is governed by the College of Primates, in descending order of precedence:

1. **See of Armagh** — Primate of Ireland, first among equals (*Archbishop Corpo of Armagh, Comharba Pátraic*)
2. **See of St. Davids** — Primate of the Welsh (*Archbishop Claude of St. Davids*)
3. **See of Canterbury** — Primate of the Anglo-Saxons (*Archbishop Ellis of Canterbury*)
4. **See of York** — Primate of the Danes, forthcoming Primate of all Northern Britania (*Archbishop Acutis of York*)
5. **Primacy of Scandinavia** — Forthcoming, to be established at the Synod of York
